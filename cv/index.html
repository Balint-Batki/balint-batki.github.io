Publications

  • 2024.05.03
    Application of machine learning methods for spent fuel characterization based on gamma spectrometry measurements
    Annals of Nuclear Energy
    We have developed cutting-edge machine learning models to predict key properties of spent nuclear fuel assemblies, including burnup, cooling time, initial enrichment, and Pu-239 content. Here's a glimpse into our approach: 🔍 Data Processing: Measured gamma spectra are analyzed, and activity ratios of fission products are calculated to serve as input features. 🤖 Models Deployed: Support Vector Regression, Random Forest, and Multi-Layer Perceptron models are used, considering data uncertainty during inference to produce prediction intervals. Input features are ranked using the Gini importance from Random Forest models. 🌟 Deep Learning Innovation: A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model has been developed to predict spent fuel parameters directly from the measured spectra. 💡 Results: These new models significantly outperform previous methods, which relied on nonlinear regression using a single feature. Our approach not only improves the precision of burnup and cooling time predictions but also accurately estimates initial enrichment and Pu-239 content.
  • 2023.11.04
    Water level prediction using long short-term memory neural network model for a lowland river: a case study on the Tisza River, Central Europe
    Environmental Sciences Europe
    State-of-the-art results in river water level prediction! 🌊 Our latest research in Hungary challenges traditional methods with cutting-edge machine learning. 🚀 After rigorous testing, the Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) model outperformed the conventional Discrete Linear Cascade Model (DLCM) across all time horizons, delivering more accurate 7-day forecasts. 📈 Despite slight underestimations, LSTM's encoder-decoder architecture achieved 68.5–76.1% precision within required intervals, particularly excelling in low and flood stages. 🌐 Our findings suggest LSTM's effectiveness in addressing climate-driven hydrological changes, urging further exploration in similar conditions.

  • 2021.01.01
    Simulation of an unprotected transient of the ALLEGRO reactor using the coupled neutronics/thermal-hydraulics system code KIKO3DMG/ATHLET3.0
    Annals of Nuclear Energy
    It is the first-ever analysis of an unprotected control rod withdrawal transient of the ALLEGRO using a coupled neutronics/thermal-hydraulics code, namely the KIKO3DMG/ATHLET. It is revealed that the KIKO3DMG/ATHLET provides good results by using 6-group constants instead of the widely used 24-group constants, and with this, the computation time of the transient is reduced by 95%. By comparative analysis, it is found that the point-kinetics approach underestimates the assembly-wise peak temperatures nearby the withdrawn control assembly during the investigated transient due to the fixed power distribution and the lack of spatial reactivity effects.
  • 2020.01.09
    Extension and application of the KIKO3DMG nodal code for fast reactor core analyses
    Annals of Nuclear Energy
    A new methodology for modeling the non-uniform axial thermal expansion of fuel with the KIKO3DMG is developed by introducing small mixed nodes. The new model is verified on the ALLEGRO reactor by comparison with Serpent Monte Carlo calculations. It is shown that, in the introduced small mixed nodes, volume-weighted group constants provide correct results, although it is essential to weight the inverse of the diffusion coefficients instead of the diffusion coefficients. It is derived that the inverse volume-weighted diffusion coefficient aims to keep the neutron current at the interface of the merged regions.

  • 2018.04.01
    Calculation of core safety parameters and uncertainty analyses during unprotected transients for the ALLEGRO and a sodium-cooled fast reactor
    Annals of Nuclear Energy
    This paper presents the first-ever best estimate plus uncertainty (BEPU) analyses of an unprotected transient overpower (UTOP) and an unprotected loss of flow transient (ULOF) of the ALLEGRO core. The uncertainties of the most important selected core safety parameters originated from possible user effects, modeling uncertainties, and nuclear data uncertainties are taken into account. The ATHLET code with point-kinetics methodology is used. It is found that the peak cladding temperature is sensitive to all the selected uncertain core safety parameters during the ULOF transient. During the UTOP transient, the Doppler and the fuel thermal expansion reactivity coefficients are the dominant factors. It is shown that the peak cladding temperature reaches unacceptable levels during the UTOP transient initiated by a potential 1$ reactivity insertion, which adumbrates that additional safety shutdown systems are to be applied to mitigate the consequences of reactivity transients.
